4. Thailand

The Grand Palace

Built in 1782, The Grand Palace is a complex of buildings at the heart of Bangkok, capital of Thailand, it has years of history and the place is used for hosting royal ceremonies and welcoming the king's guests, State guests, and other foreign dignitaries worth the visit.

Koh Kood Island

Located in the Koh Chang archipelago in the Gulf of Thailand, in-the-know visitors that make their way to Koh Kood can still walk down beautiful beaches almost alone and ride a motorbike on empty roads. it also has cheap food compared to other cities and beaches in thailand.

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is known for being home to protected habitats for the greatest number of animal species in Thailand. Its food sources attract many charismatic species which can often be viewed by visitors: wild elephant, gaur, dhole, muntjac, sambar deer, gibbon, hornbill and macaque, if you lean more towards nature then defenitely check it out!
